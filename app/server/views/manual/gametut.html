Game Tutorial

This tutorial guides the user through creating a game, entering shots and viewing the shots graphically and statistically.

Creating your first game

Return to the home screen and click the Game button. There won't be any games listed so click the plus symbol top right to create a new game. Enter the venue and opposing team's name and click save. Select the game you just created from the game list and you will be taken to the shot input screen. Note the view moves to landscape giving the highest resolution for placing shots on the court. Portrait orientation is not currently supported for the game views.

To enter a shot first select a player from the list on the left. Then click in the court area where the player took a shot from. Finally change the filters to the right of the screen to specfiy the type of shot.

To enter subsequent shots, start by selecting the player - if it's the same player no need. Enter the shot and set the filters. You can change the shot filters up to the point you select a new player or enter a new shot.

Shot Views

To see the entered shots, select the view icon in the tab bar at the bottom of the screen. Adjust the filters to the right to see the shots you are interested in. Selecting off for a filter shows all shots related to that filter, ie. if you select off for the made/miss filter, both will be shown, made shots in a green colour and missed in red.

You can show/hide both the individual shot (circles) and the shot segment split by toggling the two switches to the right, under the filters.

Send Email

You can send any shot view setup to anyone using their email address. When you have the view showing the info you want to display, click the email button. You can then enter email addresses from your contacts list by click the To: button at the top left. You can also enter a subject and a message, like a normal email. You will see a preview of the view to be sent to the right. Click Send button in the top nav bar to send the image in a pdf document format.

Shot Statistics

To view the shot segment statistics click the stats icon in the tab bar at the bottom of the screen.

Filter Terms

  • quarter - quarter list, one to four
  • made/miss - for shots made or missed
  • freethrow - specify a shot as a freethrow or normal play
  • off - shot taken after dribbling, catch or pushback
  • direction - before taking the shot, player moving left or right
  • basket - before taking the shot, player moving toward or away from the basket
